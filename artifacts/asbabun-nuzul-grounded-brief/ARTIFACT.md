# Context-Grounded Creative Brief

**Kind:** method · **Category:** business · **Status:** stable

Before you brief a creative or an LLM, capture the *specific occasion* that triggered
the need — the who, the moment, the tension — then generalize outward. Concrete
specificity is what creates universal resonance; abstract briefs produce generic output.

---

## Why this exists

Briefs that start abstract ("make it inspiring, for everyone") yield forgettable work.
The most resonant messages are grafted onto one concrete trigger — a real person, a real
moment, a real tension — and only then widened to the audience. Naming the occasion first
gives the work a spine; skipping it is the single most common cause of bland output.

## The four grounding questions

1. **Trigger** — what specific event or pain created this need *right now*?
2. **Audience** — who exactly is in the moment (not "everyone" — one person you can picture)?
3. **Moment** — where and when does the message land (platform, mindset, time of day)?
4. **Tension** — what unresolved feeling or contradiction makes them care?

Answer these in one or two sentences each, in the audience's own words. Then, and only
then, abstract to the reusable message.

## How to use

- Put the four answers at the TOP of every brief or prompt; make the model restate them
  before generating.
- Reject any concept that cannot point back to the trigger — that is the drift signal.
- Keep the specifics in the prompt even for broad campaigns; specificity transfers,
  vagueness does not.
